GENERAL INSTRUCTION:


  • All the participants are expected to come with their ID card and Bonafide Certificate.

  • No TA/DA will be provided. Refreshment and Lunch will be served.

  • The decision of the jury will be final.

  • Prelims will be conducted for all the events, if needed.

  • Participants are allowed to participate in maximum of four events, out of four one must belong to Technical event.

  • It is the responsibility of the participants to take care for not overlapping of their events.

  • Maximum of 15 participants per College is allowed.

  • Registration fee Rs. 150/- per participants, should be paid offline only.

  • Spot registration is no allowed.

  • For more details please visit your website: WWW.youthzest24.com

  • Participants List also send the Mail youthzest@vcsm.ac.in

  • Participants have to register themselves by filling the form given and send to the department on or before 30 January 2024.

      PAPER SHOW AND TELL (Paper Presentation) Topics to present:

      • Genetic algorithms

      • Virtual Reality and Augmented Reality

      • Genomics

      • Blue eyes technology

      • Edge Computing

      • Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning

      • Internet of Things (IOT)

      • Data Science

      • Human-computer interface

      • Blockchain

  • The PPT should contain 12 slides maximum and 8 slides minimum.

  • Participants have to submit their soft copy one day before through the Mail youthzest@vcsm.ac.in.

  • Decision taken by organizing team will be the final decision.

    MIND FEST (Technical Quiz)

  • Only one team of 2 members is allowed.

  • There will be a preliminary round in which 5 teams will be chosen for the final round.

  • Questions will be technical.

    CODE-D-BUG (Debugging)

  • Only one team of 2 members is allowed.

  • Totally two rounds: Preliminary Round and final Round.

  • Preliminary Round: MCQ questions from Using C / C++ / Java / Python Language.

  • The Selected students only admitted to the final round.

  • Final Round: The student should correct the bug in the program and to get the output.

    Student Coordinators - Ms. S. Megadharshini – 7010506467

    POSTER EXPO (Poster Making)

  • Only one member is allowed.

  • Only using the Design Tool „Photoshop‟.

  • Participants have to design a poster based on the theme given on the spot.

  • Internet facility will be provided.

    Student Coordinators - Mr. B. Tamil selvan - 9344242827


    CODE IN THE DARK (Blind Programming)

  • Only one member is allowed.

  • A Program will be given to you as a Hard copy.

  • Participants have to type the program in a Notepad and MONITOR SWITCHED OFF.

  • Participants, who get minimum errors, will be announced as winners.

  • Decision taken by organizing team will be the final decision.

    Student Coordinators - Ms. B. Kaviya - 9655590856


    CODE INNOVATE (Code Development)

  • Only one team of 2 members is allowed.

  • Totally two rounds: Preliminary Round and final Round.

  • Preliminary Round: MCQ questions from Using C / C++ / Java / Python Language.

  • The Selected students only admitted to the final round.

  • Final Round: The student should write the missing code in the program and to get the output.

  • Decision taken by organizing team will be the final decision.

    Student Coordinators - Mr. S. Venkatesh – 9360994122

  • Decision taken by organizing team will be the final decision.

    WHO AM I (Find Me)

  • Only one team of 2 members is allowed.

    Student Coordinators - Ms. R. Saranya - 9940842615

    PUZZLE BUILDING

  • Only one team of 2 members is allowed.

  • Complete three sets of images by finding missing pieces.

  • Start with a bowl containing incomplete sets.

  • Teams connect the puzzle, no helping.

  • Game in-charge may give hints.

  • Set a time limit for excitement.

    Student Coordinators - Mr. A. Jegan - 6382953524


    ZOMBIE CROSSING

  • Only one member play at a time is allowed.

  • There are 9 rows and 3 columns of foots and palms print.

  • A participant wants to cross one end to another end.

  • When the Participant enter into 1st row, do not take their foots and palms from the print until complete that row.

  • Participant want to place their foots and palms in the correct printings.

  • If the person place their foots and palms on the wrong printings, they are disqualified.

  • If the person take their foots and palms without completing the row, they also disqualified.

    Student Coordinators - Mr. P. Balakrishnan - 9486367128


    REEL OR REAL

  • Only one team of 2 members is allowed.

  • Two facts will be present for 60 seconds to find the real and reel.

  • Players will not communicate their answers with their team members while playing.

  • After a designated time, event host will reveal their answers.

  • Players receive points based on their correct determinations.

    Student Coordinators - Mr. K. Gokul - 9677866631
